Comparison of MusicXML and MEI

When comparing MusicXML and MEI, I found they differ most in how they structure musical information. MusicXML is more explicit and readable, using longer element names and nested structures. For example, it encodes pitch with [pitch] [step] [octave] 3 [/octave] [pitch], while MEI expresses the same data through compact attributes such as [note pname=”a” oct=”3” dur=”4”/] . MusicXML also places clef, key, and time signatures inside an attributes element within the first measure, whereas MEI centralizes these definitions in scoreDef and staffDef at the score level.

The two formats organise notation differently as well: MusicXML follows a [part]→[measure]→[note] structure, while MEI uses [measure]→[staff]→[layer]→[note]. MusicXML includes articulations as nested elements under [notations], whereas MEI encodes them more concisely as note attributes (e.g. artic="stacc").
